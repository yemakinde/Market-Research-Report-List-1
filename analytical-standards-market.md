<p><ul><li>Organic Standards</li><li>Inorganic Standards</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Analytical standards are reference materials used in laboratories to ensure accuracy and reliability of analytical measurements. There are two main market types of analytical standards: organic standards and inorganic standards. Organic standards contain carbon and are used to measure organic compounds, such as pesticides or pharmaceuticals. Inorganic standards, on the other hand, do not contain carbon and are used to measure elements or inorganic compounds, such as metals or minerals. These standards play a crucial role in quality control, research, and regulatory compliance in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, environmental testing, and food safety.</p></p>
