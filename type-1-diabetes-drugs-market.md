<p><p>The Type 1 Diabetes Drugs market primarily consists of two types: Insulins and Others. Insulins are the most commonly prescribed type 1 diabetes drugs. They are medications that help regulate blood sugar levels by replacing or supplementing the body's natural insulin production. Insulins can be classified into different types based on their duration of action, including rapid-acting, short-acting, intermediate-acting, and long-acting insulins. On the other hand, the category of Others includes various non-insulin medications such as amylin analogs, GLP-1 receptor agonists, and SGLT2 inhibitors, which are typically used in combination with insulins to further control blood sugar levels.</p></p>
