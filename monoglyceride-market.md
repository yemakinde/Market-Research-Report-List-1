<p><p>Monoglycerides are commonly used food additives that have various applications in the food industry. General monoglycerides are typically derived from vegetable oils and are used as emulsifiers, stabilizers, and thickeners in a wide range of food products. Distilled monoglycerides are produced through a process of distillation, resulting in a higher purity level compared to general monoglycerides. They are often used in bakery products, dairy items, and beverages for their emulsifying properties. Both types of monoglycerides play a crucial role in improving the texture, appearance, and shelf life of food products.</p></p>
