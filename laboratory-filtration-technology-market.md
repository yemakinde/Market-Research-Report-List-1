<p><p>Laboratory Filtration Technology is a market that encompasses various types of filtration technologies. Microfiltration Technology involves using a porous membrane to separate particles from liquids. Reverse Osmosis Technology utilizes pressure to force water through a membrane, removing impurities. Ultrafiltration Technology removes particles and macromolecules from liquids by using a membrane with smaller pores than microfiltration. Vacuum Filtration Technology applies vacuum pressure to draw liquids through a filter. Nanofiltration Technology utilizes a membrane with smaller pores than ultrafiltration to separate smaller particles and ions from liquids.</p></p>
