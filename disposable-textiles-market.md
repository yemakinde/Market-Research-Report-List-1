<p><p>The disposable textiles market can be categorized into two types: non-woven fabrics and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are created by bonding fibers together through different processes, resulting in a fabric-like material. These fabrics are durable, lightweight, and often used in products such as medical gowns, surgical masks, and wipes. On the other hand, woven fabrics are made by interlacing yarns in a crisscross pattern. These fabrics are strong and commonly found in products like bed linens, clothing, and towels. Both types serve specific purposes in the disposable textiles market, catering to different industries and applications.</p></p>
