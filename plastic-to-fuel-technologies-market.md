<p><p>Plastic-to-fuel technologies are innovative methods that convert plastic waste into usable fuel products. Pyrolysis is a market type that employs high temperatures to break down plastic into a mixture of liquid and gas, which can be further refined into fuels like gasoline and diesel. Depolymerization, on the other hand, involves chemically breaking down plastic polymers into their original monomer form, allowing them to be converted into various fuel products. Both pyrolysis and depolymerization technologies are promising solutions to reduce plastic waste and provide alternative energy sources.</p></p>
