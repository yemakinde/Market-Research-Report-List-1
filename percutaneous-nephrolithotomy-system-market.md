<p><p>Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL) system is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used for treating kidney stones. It is used to remove large or complex kidney stones that cannot be treated using non-invasive methods like shock wave lithotripsy or ureteroscopy. The procedure involves making a small incision in the patient's back and inserting a hollow tube into the kidney to remove the stones.</p><p>The market for Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy System is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of kidney stones, particularly in the aging population, is driving the market growth. According to the National Kidney Foundation, about 1 in 10 people will have a kidney stone in their lifetime. Additionally, the advancements in surgical techniques, such as the development of miniaturized and flexible instruments, have improved the success rates and outcomes of PCNL procedures.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures is also contributing to the market growth. PCNL offers several advantages over traditional open surgery, including reduced pain, shorter hospital stays, and faster recovery time. These benefits are driving the adoption of PCNL systems among patients and healthcare providers.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing focus on the development of technologically advanced PCNL systems. Manufacturers are investing in research and development activities to enhance the precision and efficiency of the procedures.
