<p><p>Fire penetration seals are used to prevent the spread of fire and smoke through openings in walls, floors, and ceilings. There are different types of seals available, including foam, block, ablative coating, and others. Foam seals use expandable foam to fill gaps and provide a barrier against fire and smoke. Block seals use blocks of fire-resistant materials to create a barrier. Ablative coating seals involve applying a special coating that swells up when exposed to heat, creating a protective barrier. Other types of seals may include intumescent wraps or collars.</p></p>
