<p><p>Byler disease, also known as progressive familial intrahepatic cholestasis type 1 (PFIC1), is a rare genetic disorder that affects the liver's ability to secrete bile. Byler disease is usually diagnosed in infancy or early childhood, and symptoms include jaundice, itching, poor growth, and vitamin deficiencies.</p><p>The treatment for Byler disease focuses on managing symptoms and preventing complications. This typically involves medications to increase bile flow, fat-soluble vitamin supplements, and nutritional support.
