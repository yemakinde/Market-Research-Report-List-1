<p><p>Mechanical pulps are categorized into two main types: Hardwood Mechanical Pulp (HMP) and Softwood Mechanical Pulp (SMP), each derived from different sources of trees. HMP is made from deciduous trees such as birch or aspen, resulting in a pulp with high brightness and good opacity. On the other hand, SMP is produced from coniferous trees like spruce or pine, yielding a pulp with high tensile strength and stiffness. Both types of mechanical pulps have their own distinct characteristics and are used in various paper and packaging applications.</p></p>
