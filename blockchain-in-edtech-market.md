<p><p>In the EdTech market, private blockchain refers to a decentralized system where only authorized participants can access and validate transactions. Public blockchain, on the other hand, allows anyone to participate in the system, making it transparent and secure. Consortium blockchain is a hybrid model, involving multiple organizations working together to control and operate the network. Each market type offers unique advantages; private blockchain ensures data privacy, public blockchain promotes transparency, while consortium blockchain fosters collaboration among stakeholders in the EdTech industry.</p></p>
