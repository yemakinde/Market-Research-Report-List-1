<p><p>Glued Laminated Timbers (Glulam) and Cross Laminated Timbers (CLT) are engineered wood products that offer several advantages over traditional solid wood materials. </p><p>Glulam is created by bonding together multiple layers of dimensioned lumber using structural adhesive. This process enhances the strength and stiffness of the timber, making it highly suitable for various structural applications such as beams, columns, and arches. Its versatility, aesthetic appeal, and sustainable nature have been driving its demand in the construction industry.</p><p>On the other hand, CLT is composed of several layers of solid wood boards stacked in alternating directions and bonded together with structural adhesives. CLT panels provide exceptional strength, stability, and fire resistance, making them an ideal choice for constructing walls, floors, and roofs in mid-rise and high-rise buildings. Its modular and prefabricated nature allows for faster construction, reducing labor costs and overall project timelines.</p><p>The Glulam and CLT market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years. The increasing emphasis on sustainable construction practices, rising demand for environmentally friendly and energy-efficient buildings, and the shift towards prefabricated building systems are some of the key factors driving this growth.
